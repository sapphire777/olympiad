PSMO, JSMO & SSMO

Open to Standard/Grade 5 to 7, Form 1 to 3 & Form 4 to A Levels respectively.

How it works

Competition Structure

The competition questions are sent to participating schools, and students sit for the contest in their own schools. The competitions consist of two rounds:

First Round (early February) : Students attempt 20 multiple-choice questions, with a time limit of 60 minutes.

Second Round (end of February) : Open to contestants who scored 50% and above in the first round, this round consists of short answer questions. Students have 60 minutes to complete the paper.

Marking : Teachers mark the papers for both rounds in their respective schools, according to the provided solution guide.

March

Mathematics Collaborative Contest (MCC)

To ensure a thrilling and educational experience, registered schools will receive detailed information about the competition format and rules. On the day of the event, each school will be assigned a well-trained guide to assist contestants and explain the rules at each stage.

How it works

Collaborative Contest Competition Format

Team Phase ( 30 minutes )
In this collaborative round, the four team members work together to solve mathematical problems within the given time. Clear justifications are required for full credit.

Relay Phase ( 30 minutes )
The relay phase features relay-style questions with four parts. Like runners in a 4x100m track relay, team members complete their part and pass the solution to the next contestant. The final contestant submits the completed solution to the invigilator. This phase consists of three rounds of questions.

Sprint Phase ( 10-15 minutes )
In this exhilarating race against time, each team member must complete 10 multiple-choice questions within 10-15 minutes. At the start signal, contestants sprint to their assigned station to pick up a problem, then return to their workstation to solve it. The final score for each team will be the combined performance across all three phases.

September

Botswana Mathematics Festival (BMF)

The BMF offers students a one-of-a-kind opportunity to explore the wonders of mathematics through a variety of engaging activities. Participating schools will receive detailed information about the event format, rules, and guidelines.

How it works

BMF Activities Overview

Math Quiz Showdown ( 1 hour )
Put your mathematical knowledge to the test in this fast-paced quiz competition. Teams will face off against each other, answering a series of questions that cover various topics in mathematics. Speed, accuracy, and teamwork are key to emerging as the ultimate math quiz champions.

Math Poetry Slam ( 45 minutes )
Unleash your creativity and express your passion for mathematics through poetry. Participants will compose and perform original math-themed poems, showcasing their artistic talents and appreciation for the subject. Poems will be judged based on creativity, originality, and relevance to the theme.

Math Debate Challenge ( 1 hour 30 minutes )
Engage in stimulating debates on intriguing mathematical topics. Teams will be assigned a stance on a pre-selected topic and will present persuasive arguments to defend their position. Debates will be judged based on clarity, logical reasoning, and presentation skills.

Creative Math Problem Solving ( 1 hour )
Think outside the box as you tackle unconventional math problems in this unique challenge. Teams will work together to develop innovative solutions to complex problems, demonstrating their adaptability, creativity, and collaborative skills.
